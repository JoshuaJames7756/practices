📐 Present Simple — When & Why / Cuándo y Por Qué

✅ We use Present Simple for routines and habits / Lo usamos para rutinas y hábitos

  • She wakes up at 6:30 every morning. (rutina diaria)
  • He studies English on Mondays. (hábito semanal)
  • They always eat breakfast together. (hábito con adverbio)

✅ Affirmative / ❌ Negative / ❓ Question — Formas

FormStructureExample (He)
✅ Affirmative Subject + verb+s + … He brushes his teeth.
❌ Negative Subject + doesn't + base verb + … He doesn't brush his teeth. (NO doesnt brushes!)
❓ Question Does + subject + base verb + …? Does he brush his teeth?
⚠️ Errores comunes de Latinos
She doesn't eats breakfast. ❌
She doesn't eat breakfast. ✅
Después de "doesn't", el verbo NO lleva -s.
Does he goes to school? ❌
Does he go to school? ✅
En preguntas con "Does", el verbo vuelve a su forma base.
He study English every day. ❌
He studies English every day. ✅
study termina en consonante + y → cambia a -ies para he/she/it.
⏱ Tiempo sugerido: 10 min — Lee el texto en voz alta primero. Luego pide a los estudiantes que subrayen los verbos en 3ra persona. Finalmente, responden las preguntas en parejas.
📄 Reading — Carlos's Day

This is Carlos. He is 15 years old and he is a student. Carlos wakes up at 6:30 AM every day. He gets up quickly and goes to the bathroom. Carlos brushes his teeth and takes a cold shower. After that, he gets dressed and has breakfast. He usually eats cereal and drinks orange juice.


Carlos leaves home at 7:15 AM and takes the bus to school. He studies five different subjects. His favorite is English! At 12:00 PM, he has lunch with his friends in the cafeteria.


In the afternoon, Carlos does his homework and watches videos on his phone. He plays football with his friends from 5 to 6 PM. In the evening, he helps his mom cook dinner. After dinner, Carlos reads for 20 minutes and then goes to bed at 10:00 PM. He never stays up very late on school days.

Los verbos en azul y subrayados están en tercera persona (he/she/it) → Present Simple con -s
❓ Comprehension Questions
  1. What time does Carlos wake up every day?
  2. What does Carlos eat and drink for breakfast?
  3. How does Carlos get to school?
  4. What does Carlos do from 5 to 6 PM?
  5. What time does Carlos go to bed? Does he stay up late on school days?
👩‍🏫 Extensión: Pregunta a los estudiantes: "What's different between Carlos's routine and your routine?" — respuestas orales breves en inglés o con ayuda del vocabulario de la sección anterior.

Model Dialogue — Diálogo Modelo
A:What time do you wake up on school days?
B:I wake up at 6:45. It's very early! What about you?
A:I wake up at 7:00. Do you eat breakfast?
B:Yes, I do. I usually eat toast and drink coffee. Do you?
A:No, I don't. I never eat in the morning!
B:Really? What do you do after school?
A:I usually play video games for one hour. Then I do my homework.
B:Cool! I play football with my friends. I go to bed at 10:30.

✍️ Writing Task — My Daily Routine

Task: Write 6–8 sentences about your own daily routine. Use the sentence starters below as a guide.

📝 Sentence Starters — Inicios de Oraciones
  • I wake up at ___ every day. / I usually wake up early / late.
  • In the morning, I ___ and then I ___.
  • I have breakfast at ___ . / I don't eat breakfast.
  • I go to school at ___ . / I take the bus / walk.
  • After school, I always / usually ___ .
  • In the evening, I ___ and ___.
  • I go to bed at ___ . I never / sometimes ___.
📋 Your Writing — Tu Redacción
💡 Recuerda: cuando hablas de ti mismo usa la forma base (I wake up). La -s solo es para he/she/it.
👩‍🏫 Criterios de evaluación sugeridos: ✅ Usa al menos 6 verbos de rutina · ✅ Usa adverbios de frecuencia (always, usually, never) · ✅ Aplica correctamente el Present Simple · ✅ Incluye expresiones de tiempo

🔑 Answer Key — Clave de Respuestas

Activity 1 — Vocabulary Match

  1. wake up → despertarse
  2. brush teeth → cepillarse los dientes
  3. get dressed → vestirse
  4. have lunch → almorzar
  5. do homework → hacer la tarea
  6. go to bed → irse a la cama

Activity 2 — Fill in the Blanks

  1. Maria wakes up at 6 o'clock every morning.
  2. He brushes his teeth after breakfast.
  3. She has lunch at school with her friends.
  4. Carlos goes to bed at 10 PM.
  5. My brother watches TV in the evening.
  6. She studies English every Tuesday.
  7. He plays football on weekends.
  8. Laura gets dressed after her shower.

Activity 3 — Unscramble

  1. She always wakes up at 7 o'clock.
  2. He never eats breakfast in the morning.
  3. Does she go to school by bus?
  4. My sister studies English every day.

Activity 4 — Error Correction

  1. She don't → She doesn't eat breakfast.
  2. He studys → He studies every day.
  3. Does she goes → Does she go to school?
  4. I wakes up → I wake up at 7:00.

Reading — Comprehension Answers

  1. Carlos wakes up at 6:30 AM.
  2. He eats cereal and drinks orange juice.
  3. He takes the bus.
  4. He plays football with his friends.
  5. He goes to bed at 10:00 PM. No, he doesn't stay up late on school days.

Exit Ticket

  1. She brushes her teeth. (+ es rule: sh/ch → +es)
  2. He doesn't eat lunch. (negative = doesn't + base verb)
  3. Does he wake up early? (question = Does + subject + base verb)
